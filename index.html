Key Rules
⏱️ The DAO Timing Rule
Take DAO enzyme 15–20 minutes before eating — not with the meal. It must be present in the gut before histamine-containing food (especially sardines) arrives. This single timing change dramatically improves its effectiveness.
💧 The Fluid Divorce Rule
All therapeutic liquids — Glutamine, Magnesium, Liposomal C — taken strictly 30 minutes away from solid food. Fat-soluble supplements stay with the sardine meal. Everything else waits.
🐟 The Sardine Strategy
Sardines are not just food — they are the fat-delivery vehicle for all fat-soluble supplements. Gotu Kola, Astaxanthin, Lutein/Zeaxanthin, Horse Chestnut, Quercetin, and Sea Buckthorn all need dietary fat to absorb properly.
🌿 One New Supplement Per Week
Introduce supplements one at a time, one week apart. If there is any reaction, you will know exactly what caused it. Priority order: Gotu Kola → Bilberry → Rutin → Ashwagandha → Astaxanthin → Lutein/Zeaxanthin.
☕ Coffee & CSR
Caffeine raises cortisol and is a documented trigger for CSR worsening. Switching to green tea (which contains L-theanine to offset the caffeine) or herbal tea is one of the highest-impact single changes for the retinal condition.
🌙 Sleep = Repair
Vascular and retinal repair happens during deep sleep. The evening ashwagandha + magnesium glycinate + L-theanine combination is designed to naturally improve sleep depth. Better sleep usually precedes visible improvement in petechiae and vision.
Foods to Prioritise
  • ✓Buckwheat (gluten-free, rich in rutin — direct capillary repair)
  • ✓Blueberries / bilberries (anthocyanins for retinal and vascular health)
  • ✓Pumpkin seeds (zinc + magnesium — supports supplement protocol)
  • ✓Olive oil or coconut oil (safe fat sources for supplement absorption — zero amine content)
  • ✓Turmeric + black pepper (potent anti-inflammatory — add to sardines)
  • ✓Chamomile or passionflower tea (evening cortisol reduction)
Foods to Reduce or Avoid
  • ✗Coffee and strong tea (cortisol spike, CSR trigger)
  • ✗Avocado (high in dopamine and serotonin — significant amine load, use olive oil or coconut oil instead)
  • ✗Alcohol, aged cheeses, processed meats (high histamine)
  • ✗High-sodium processed foods (increases lower-limb pressure, worsens petechiae)
  • ✗Any gluten — 100% strict, always
B12 Serum Target

Request serum B12 every 3–6 months. Target: above 400 pmol/L for retinal and neurological protection. Many labs flag 'normal' at much lower levels — aim higher. If below 400, increase sublingual dose consistently toward 1 ml daily. Confirm form is methylcobalamin.
